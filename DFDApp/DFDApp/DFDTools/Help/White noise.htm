White noise

White noise is a random signal (or process) with a flat power spectral density.

In statistical sense, a time series  is characterized as having weak white noise if   is a sequence of serially uncorrelated random variables with zero mean and finite variance. Strong white noise also has the quality of being independent and identically distributed, which implies no autocorrelation. In particular, if  is normally distributed with mean zero and standard deviation σ , the series is called a Gaussian white noise.
